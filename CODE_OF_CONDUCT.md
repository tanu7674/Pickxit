# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

SnapID Studio is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful community for all contributors and users, regardless of:

- Age, body size, or disability
- Ethnicity or national origin
- Gender identity or expression
- Level of experience
- Education or socioeconomic status
- Nationality, personal appearance, or race
- Religion or political views
- Sexual identity or orientation

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

We encourage:
- Being kind and constructive in communication
- Respecting differing opinions and experiences
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- Focusing on discussions, not individuals
- Acknowledging and celebrating contributions
- Helping others when you can
- Asking for and giving feedback respectfully

### Unacceptable Behavior

We do not tolerate:
- Harassment, bullying, or intimidation
- Discrimination of any kind
- Unwelcome sexual advances or attention
- Personal attacks or insulting language
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Trolling, spam, or off-topic content
- Threats of violence or hostile behavior
- Abuse of power or authority

## Reporting & Enforcement

### Reporting a Violation

If you witness or experience a violation:

1. **Document the incident** — Screenshot, link, or note details
2. **Report to maintainers** — Email: conduct@snapid.app (placeholder)
3. **Provide context** — What happened, who was involved, when

**We take all reports seriously and will investigate promptly.**

### Confidentiality

- Reports are handled confidentially
- Reporter identity is protected
- Details are shared only with those investigating

### Consequences

Violations may result in:
- Warning or request for apology
- Temporary suspension from discussions
- Permanent ban from community
- Removal of content

**Severity matters.** Minor missteps may warrant a warning; serious violations may result in immediate bans.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub issues and discussions
- Pull request conversations
- Email communications with maintainers
- Social media posts about the project
- In-person meetups or events
- Any space representing SnapID Studio

## Examples

### What We Consider Respectful

```
"I disagree with your approach because [reasons]. Here's an alternative: [suggestion]"

"I'm new to this, can someone help me understand how this works?"

"Great idea! Here's how we could improve it: [feedback]"

"Thank you for the fix! This really helps the project."
```

### What We Don't Tolerate

```
"That's a stupid idea."

"You don't know what you're talking about."

"[Derogatory comment about person]"

"Your contribution isn't welcome here because of [protected characteristic]"
```

## Special Notes

### For Newcomers

- Don't worry about being perfect
- It's okay to ask questions
- We all started as beginners
- We're here to help

### For Contributors

- Lead by example
- Call out bad behavior respectfully
- Thank people for their work
- Mentor others when you can

### For Maintainers

- Enforce this code consistently
- Respond to reports promptly
- Keep discussions civil
- Model the behavior we want to see

This Code of Conduct may be updated as the community grows. Changes will be:
- Discussed in GitHub discussions
- Voted on by maintainers
- Announced in a changelog entry
- Effective 30 days after announcement
